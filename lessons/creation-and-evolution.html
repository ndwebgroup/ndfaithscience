Creation Reveals the Presence of God

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson plan is designed to use the story of Genesis to instill an understanding that faith does not undermine science and science does not undermine faith. By using images of nature, students will gain an appreciation for the spark of wonder and awe that can come from marveling at Gods creation. Students will then apply their newfound understanding to a creative project that includes a reflection which describes their experience of wonder and awe regarding creation.

Creation and Evolution: Can these two perspectives coexist?

  • Edit
  • Delete
The purpose of the Lesson Plan is to have students explore the concepts of Creation through Genesis 1. Students will develop an understanding that through prior knowledge of Darwins Theory of Evolution that Faith and Science do not contradict one another. In actuality both concepts work together to compliment and give a full understanding of Gods complete participation in Creation by identifying primary and secondary causality. Students will be able to articulate why Genesis 1 and Darwins theory of evolution are not contradictory.

  • Delete
This is a lesson that introduces the Creation account of Genesis and the Theory of Evolution showing that the two are compatible. There are short readings for homework, class discussions based on the reading, small group discussion, a short Natural Selection activity, and a final discussion leading students to the conclusion that the two are compatible.

Evolution: For the Bible Tells Me So

  • Edit
  • Delete
We are creating three 50-minute lessons (with 2 science lessons beforehand for prior knowledge) that will fit at the end of the 9th grade year in Biology and correlate with the 9th grade Introduction to the Bible class. In this cross-curricular lesson, the Bible class will revisit the Genesis account now having been through a survey of the Hebrew and Christian Scriptures. Developmentally, we hope the end of the year 9s will be more ready to see connections across departments and that they will not be as influenced by a rote or literal reading of the Creation account. These lessons will serve as an introduction to the topic of evolution from both the religious and scientific perspective, and may lead further to a cross-departmental elective on the topic.

Evolution and Catholicism: The Intersection of Faith and Science

  • Edit
  • Delete
The purpose of this lesson is to begin a dialogue with students about the conflict between Faith and Science before entering a study of biological evolution. After a brief introductory lecture about the Churchs relationship with science, including consideration of primary and secondary causality, students will engage with primary Church sources and articulate the Churchs position on biological evolution. This is designed for a 90 minute block period but could easily be adapted for a 45-50 minute class. (Nolan Catholic High School)

RELATING THE DOCTRINE OF CREATION AND THE THEORY OF EVOLUTION

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson will be covered in 2- 80 minute Freshmen Theology class and 2-80 minute Freshmen Biology classes, with separate assessment portions. This lesson explores the relationship of the doctrine of creation and the Earths history of events. In theology, freshmen students will explore Genesis Chapter 1. The goal of the lesson will be to look at the time in the Bible and the progression from disorder and chaos to order and harmony and the days of creation will impact the other events of the other days in creation. Biology will examine the geologic time scale in order to observe the slow and varied pace of events as well as their relatedness. Through engagement in the lessons from both subject areas, the student will be taking a look as the connections rather than the contradictions of creation and evolution.

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson explores the scientific understanding of the evolution of the cosmos and the whole of history as the timeless act of Gods Divine Creation. During this activity, students will be spaced out along a football field (or equivalent area) in spaces representing events in cosmological time, geological time, evolutionary time, human history, and salvation history.

Understanding God's Ongoing Creation

  • Edit
  • Delete
This 9th grade biology lesson will help students discern that scientific reason does not conflict with the ongoing development of creation. The first thing Sacred Scripture tells us about God is that He created: In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth Gen 1:1. This is an essential article of belief among Christians. Scripture reveals that the Christian Doctrine of Creation contains four elements that reveal creation is an ongoing process. Some of the processes of creation can be understood through scientific inquiry.
