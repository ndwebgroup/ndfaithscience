The Theory of Evolution and the Creation Account of Genesis

  • Edit
  • Delete
This is a lesson that introduces the Creation account of Genesis and the Theory of Evolution showing that the two are compatible. There are short readings for homework, class discussions based on the reading, small group discussion, a short Natural Selection activity, and a final discussion leading students to the conclusion that the two are compatible.
Very good

1 votes with an average rating of 5.

Essential Questions

1. What are the implications of a proper understanding of literary genre found in the creation accounts?

2. What are the main tenets of the Theory of Evolution by Natural Selection? 

3. When viewed through the proper lens of science and the Catholic teaching, how does the modern Theory of Evolution by Natural Selection interface with the creation accounts in Genesis?  

 

Knowledge

  1. Understand key concepts of the Creation account in Genesis 
  2. Explain common points of misunderstanding of both the Theory and Creation
  3. Describe the Theory of Evolution and how natural selection works
  4. Clearly explain what specifically makes humans different than other animals

Skills

The student will be able to:

  1. State the Theory of Evolution by Natural Selection
  2. Understand the basics of the Creation account in Genesis
  3. Comprehend the type of genre of the Creation account in Genesis and the literal sense of the Theory of Evolution
  4. Differentiate between what the Theory actually explains versus common misconceptions
  5. Evaluate complex texts and make conclusions about the meanings of the creation account and the Theory
  6. Discuss and debate the apparent contradictions in the Theory and the Creation account
  7. Explain how a proper understanding of each can create a better understanding of humanity and nature

Method

Lesson 1: 90 mins

  1. Prior to Lesson, assign as homework: "Survival of the Sickest" Chapter 1, found in Files.

Day of the Lesson

  1. Pre Assessment (10 mins)

Warm up: Students write a response to these questions and be prepared to discuss them.

  1. Explain, in your own words, the Theory of Evolution by natural selection as proposed by Charles Darwin?
  2. List at least 3 things you know about human evolution.
  3. What do the Creation accounts in Genesis state?
  4. If something is true, what does that mean?

Small Group Discussion (10 mins)

  1. Review the Pre assessment as a group
  2. On the board, write down commonalities discovered in the small group discussion
  3. Go around the room to discuss the common themes uncovered

3. Based on responses, teacher goes over misconceptions and areas of sustainment

Look for the following misconceptions:

  • The Theory of Evolution is only a theory and a weak explanation
  • Humans evolved from monkeys, chimps, or modern primates
  • The Creation accounts are literal stories of the beginning of the universe

Creation Story Emphasis

4. USCCB Understanding the Bible

  1. Genres in the Bible
  2. What is a myth?
  3. Explain Genesis as symbolic cosmogony
  4. Write your own Cosmogony
  5. Understand uniqueness of Creation accounts

 

Theory of Evolution Emphasis

5. “Survival of the Sickest” book discussion

  1. Formative assessment over Ch 1 of book found in Files
  2. What specifically demonstrates natural selection in the story of hemochromatosis? How is this a model of how a population evolves?
  3. Why did hemochromatosis become more common throughout Europe during the Middle Ages?
  4. Explain how a trait can increase survival and reproduction (fitness)
  5. What could be other examples of natural selection that you know from your own experience?

 

Phet Activity - Use the Natural Selection Lab found in Files.

Post Activity Discussion:

6. Write a dialogue:

  1. Write a dialogue about the apparent conflict between the Genesis creation account and the scientific theory of human evolution.
  2. Use three characters:
    1. Danny – A high school student who believes that Darwin’s theory of evolution by natural selection has made the Genesis account into nonsense
    2. Steve – A high school student who believes that Scripture is must be read literally, and that the plain meaning of Scripture must be believed even if it means denying the clear consensus of science
    3. Tim – A high school student who explains how reading Genesis as symbolic cosmogony and understanding evolution correctly can lead to a deeper understanding of science and appreciation of faith

 

By the end, students must understand the following:

  1. Literal vs metaphorical 
  2. Natural selection is the main driving force of Evolution.
  3. Living thing will follow the rules of Natural Selection.
  4. Science does not tell us why the rules exist so we must find it philosophically and theologically.

Assessment

Survival of the Sickest Chapter 1 Formative Assessment 

1.      Aran Gordon’s blood tests eventually showed that:

a.      He was infected with Malaria

b.      He had massive mounts of iron in his blood and liver

c.      He had a genetic disease that made his blood cells misshapen

d.      He had too little sugar in his blood

 

2.      How does someone get Hemochromatosis?

a.      It is transferred by mosquitos

b.      It is through airborne infection

c.  It is genetically inherited

d.      It is due to bad life choices in diet

 

3.      Weinberg found that:

a.  Iron helps almost all bacteria to multiply nearly unrestricted

b.      Excess sugar in the blood causes weaknesses in the capillaries

c.      Dangerous bacteria can be transferred by people coughing and sneezing

d.      Certain mosquitos were responsible for the infections

 

4.      The bubonic plague:

a.      Killed every person that it infected

b.  Killed many but not all people that it infected

c.      Had little to no effect on the populations

d.      Had a slight effect on those that were infected

 

5.      Who was at the highest risk of the bubonic plague?

a.  Young men

b.      Very old men

c.      Women

d.      Children

 

6.      In chapter 1, the author references a common medical treatment in the 18th and 19th century, which was also done to George Washington was:

a.      Antibiotics

b.  Blood letting and bleeding

c.      Taking anti venoms

d.      Taking extra vitamins and home remedies

 

7.      Which of the following statements is correct according to chapter 1?

a.      If you have hemochromatosis, there is no known treatment or method of caring for it

b.      Bacteria and diseases do not require iron to survive and reproduce

c.      At the time of the middle ages, hemochromatosis was a major pandemic that killed 1/3 of the European population

d.  During the bubonic plague, those with hemochromatosis were very likely to survive

Standards

Demonstrate critical thinking through writing

            DOK Level 4 - Prove
