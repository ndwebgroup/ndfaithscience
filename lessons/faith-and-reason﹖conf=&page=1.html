Using Natural Philosophy to Develop a Bridge Between Science and Theology

  • Edit
  • Delete
As a lesson for the beginning of the school year, no assumed background in philosophy or relevant subject matter. Through Natural Philosophy students overcome a perceived divide between science and theology. Using Aristotles Four Causes, students explore how the physical universe is not the result of Gods creating particular laws rather God as the author of both the laws used by science which govern the development of nature and the very substance of nature itself.
(Detroit Cristo Rey High School)

Way of knowing: Faith and Science

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson is for freshman students taking Biology and Scripture concurrently. The intent is to provide students with a framework of the nature of science and the nature of faith and an understanding that they are not conflict. Galileo and the Catholic Church: Learning through humility.

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson aims at learning the truth of the complicated conflict between the Catholic Church and Galileo. Students will discuss proper solutions to the problems that occurred during this time. Students will attempt to reconcile Scripture with scientific theory and the development of both. Through the telling of the story historically, students will recognize faults of pride on both sides of the argument and the necessity to approach all doctrines with humility.

The Scientific Human and The Human Scientist

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson introduces students to the scientific method and its philosophical presuppositions. After presenting the steps of the scientific method (observation, hypothesis and experiment), teachers guide the students in a philosophical reflection on the uniquely human character of science, or the role played in the practice of science by passion, faith, freedom and morality. The goal is to increase scientific literacy and, at the same time, show the importance of philosophy and theology for good science.
The lesson is designed to allow teachers of different age groups and in science classes of varying disciplines to adapt the scaffold to their teaching style and classroom needs.

Morality and Emerging Technologies

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson will engage students in the relationship between science and religion as it relates to the morality of emerging technologies. The lessons (including articles, videos, discussion and written reflection) will inform students research projects, which are the final assessments for this unit. By researching emerging technology, students will deepen their understanding of the Catholic Churchs position on science and religion and the moral implications of developing specific emerging technology. The lesson is intended for upper level high school students in a Morality class.
