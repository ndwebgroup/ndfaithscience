Jesus as the New Adam, Jesus as the First Atom

  • Edit
  • Delete
For Grades 10-12 (Recommended)
This lesson presents students with questions regarding the origins of the universe and the act of Creation through God the Father and his Son, Jesus, and it challenges students to consider how science and faith intersect to answer those questions. Students evaluate possible scientific explanations for the circumstances preceding the Big Bang, analyze relevant Scripture, and articulate their thoughts in response to the evidence presented.

Essential Questions

  1. How do faith and science contribute to our understanding of the beginning of time? 
    1.  In the beginning...What was before “The beginning”?
    2.  What existed before the Big Bang? 
  2. How does the role of Jesus in creation inform our understanding of the material world?
    1. What was Jesus’s role in Creation?
      1. Jesus as the first “atom”
    2. Why would the infinite God choose to take on physical form?
      1. Connection between infinite and finite
      2. How to reconcile incarnation of Jesus with Creation/Creator God 
      3. Where would we be if God had not taken on material form?
  3. Did God need to create? Why did God create? 

Knowledge

Prerequisite Knowledge
Students should have prior knowledge of the Big Bang Theory presented in the context of a science course.
Students should have prior knowledge of the Incarnation presented in the context of a theology course.
Students should have prior knowledge of the Trinitarian doctrine of the Church presented in the context of a theology course.
 

Knowledge

1. Describe possible circumstances that preceded the Big Bang.

2. Compare and contrast between infinite and finite.

3. Describe the role of Jesus in Creation.

4. Articulate that God’s creation was a complete act of love.

5. Argue that Jesus as the New Adam brings about a New Creation.

 

Skills

  1. Evaluation of various scientific theories.
  2. Analysis of Biblical text.
  3. Dialogue with colleagues.
  4. Curiosity and imagination.
  5. Assessing and analysing information.
  6. Effective communication.

Method

Day 1 (Context of a Science Class) 50 minutes

  • How do scientists explain the origin of the universe?
    • Use Pre-Quiz to assess student knowledge of Big Bang.
      • Google Doc - Pre-quiz: Student knowledge of Big Bang
  •  What is the Big Bang Theory? 
    • Review premise of Big Bang theory via brief discussion or using video below.
      • Origins of the Universe 101 | National Geographic
      • Address student misconceptions from pre-quiz.
  • What existed before the Big Bang?
    • Pose question for students and explain challenges of answering the question.
    • Discuss possible answers to question.
      • Optional video: What happened before the Big Bang?
    • Students record brief notes: Google Doc - What happened before the Big Bang
  • What do we do with this information?
    • Think, Pair, Share: Have students record their own reactions to today’s discussion; encourage them to consider the implications of these theories in the greater context of finding meaning in life.
      • Students pair up and discuss their reactions. Select a few students to share with the class. Discuss.
    • Exit Ticket: Padlet - How do you think God fits into this?
      • Teachers - Make a copy (Remake) of this padlet to use as a template for your own work.
      • Use this question to prepare students for the next lesson.
      • Select and discuss responses.

 

Day 2 (Context of a Theology Class) 50 minutes

Opening Prayer Isaiah 60:1-3

  • Google Slides - Before the Beginning Start with Day 2 of the Google SlideShow.
  • Present the learning essential question: What is the difference between the act of creating and being creative? (10 Min)
    • Students pair up in pairs or small groups and develop an answer to the above question.  Students record answers/thoughts in a notebook to be shared for later.
  • Student demonstration to define the word “create.”
    • Select two students to come before the class and give each a box.  One box contains modeling clay and the other box will remain empty. Without telling students the contents of the boxes, instruct each student to create a bowl using the contents of their box.  Obviously one student will be able to create a bowl while the other student will not. Use this lighthearted activity to demonstrate the definition of creation and explain that humans cannot create something out of nothing. (5 Min)
  • What do the Scriptures say about Jesus’ role in Creation? (30 Min)
    • Students explore assigned Scriptures and write 2-3 sentence summaries.
      • Genesis 1: 26
      • Colossians 1:16-17 
      • John 1:1, 3, 14
      • Hebrews 7:3
    • Move between groups to assist and monitor progress.
    • Next merge all the groups to discuss findings and how they shape understanding of Jesus before creation.
  • Exit Ticket - Padlet: Jesus as the New Adam, Jesus as the First Atom
    • Students answer questions on Padlet with thoughts from lesson.
      • In the beginning...What was before “The beginning”?
      • What existed before the big bang?
    • Select and discuss responses.
    • Teachers - Make a copy (Remake) of this padlet to use as a template for your own work.

 

Day 3 (Context of a Theology Class) 50 minutes

Begin with Prayer, The Canticle of Creatures. Canticle of the Creatures

  • Google Slides - Before the Beginning Start with Day 3 of the Google SlideShow.
  • Present learning essential questions: Did God need to create? Why did God create?  (Approx 10 Min)
    • Students discuss questions in pairs/small groups.  Students write answers/thoughts in a notebook. Select students to share and discuss with class.
    • Present Church teachings: “God had no need to create…”(CCC 293).
  • So why then did God create?
    • Give time for students to discuss.
      • God is complete without creation; He does not need creation.
      •  “God created all things ‘not to increase His glory [for that is impossible] but to show it forth and communicate it’”135 (CCC 293). 
    • Creation is an act of love
      • Why did He do it? The motive for this sharing of His glory is pure unselfish love. “God has no other reason for creating than His love and goodness” (CCC 293).
  • So why was Jesus sent by the Father?
    • Students discuss in small groups, then bring ideas to large group discussion. facilitated by the teacher.
    • Present Church teaching. God’s love culminates in His act of salvation, the redemption of humanity. In this act, Jesus becomes the 2nd Adam.
    • This is the beginning of the New Creation.
  • Closing Padlet: What does Jesus as the New Adam mean for all of humanity?
    • Teachers - Remake - Make a copy of this padlet to use as a template for your own work.
  • Cumulative Assignment:
    • Assign students to write 1-2 paragraphs in response to the following questions:
      • What is your opinion about creation? Give evidence and reasons to support your opinion from both of the domains of science and theology.
      • In answering the question above, which domain (science or theology) do you find more compelling? Give reasons to support your answer.
        • Writing Prompt and Grading Rubric (Includes scaffolded notes page). 
      • This may be used as a cross-curricular summative assessment. Students analyze and evaluate scientific and theological explanations for the beginning of time.

Assessment

Day 1

  • Google Doc - Pre-quiz: Student knowledge of Big Bang - Assess student knowledge of Big Bang. Quiz responses will be used to inform instruction on Big Bang Theory and guide class discussion.
  • Class discussion and individual student conferencing - Assess student understanding of Big Bang theory and possible theories surrounding circumstances preceding Big Bang.
  • Think, Pair, Share - Students identify and share personal reactions to class discussion.
  • Padlet - How do you think God fits into this? - Use student responses to prepare students for upcoming lessons.

 

Day 2

  • Class discussion and individual student conferences - Assess student understanding of creation, relevant Scriptures, and Jesus’s role in Creation.
  • Padlet: Jesus as the New Adam, Jesus as the First Atom - Use student responses to gauge understanding of lesson objectives.

 

Day 3

  • Class discussion and individual student conferences - Assess student understanding of Church teachings regarding God’s intention in Creation and Jesus’s purpose in coming.
  • Padlet: What does Jesus as the New Adam mean for all of humanity? - Use student responses to gauge understanding of lesson objectives.

Writing Prompt and Grading Rubric (Includes scaffolded notes page) - This may be used as a cross-curricular summative assessment. Students analyze and evaluate scientific and theological explanations for the beginning of time.
