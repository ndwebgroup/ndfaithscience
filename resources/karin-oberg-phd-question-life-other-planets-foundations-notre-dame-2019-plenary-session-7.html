Dr. Ã–berg's talk focuses on the question of extraterrestrial life from scientific and theological perspectives. She discusses:

  • The nature of the issue of extraterrestrial life, and the two ways in which it raises potentially difficult questions for theology: (1) the specialness of human creation, and (2) the status of the Incarnation and of Mary as Mother of God
  • Methods scientists use for searching for potentially habitable planets
  • The characteristics that scientists look for in trying to identify habitable planets
  • The reasons that water is taken to be such a crucial condition for life
  • The history of the formation of life on earth
  • Scientists' theories of how life emerged out of chemical processes; in particular, the transition from prebiotic chemistry to RNA-based life
  • The process of planet formation
  • The future of telescopic exploration 
  • Places in our solar system we could look for signs of life, particularly, Saturn's moon Enceladus 
  • The place of humanity in the universe, and the theological implications either of us being the sole rational creatures in the universe or of there being multiple species of rational creatures
