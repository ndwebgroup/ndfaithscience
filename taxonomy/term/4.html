The Big Bang and Biblical accounts of creation

  • Edit
  • Delete
In this lesson, students will consider whether the Big Band and Biblical accounts of creation are ultimately compatible. They will examine writings from a biblical literalist, a popular "anti-theist" physicist, and a leading Catholic scientist. Students will learn that the Catholic Church affirms all legitimate science, including the Big Bang.

Biblical Creation and the Big Bang: Is there a conflict?

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson is aimed at dispelling the supposed conflict between Biblical Creation and the Big Bang Theory. Students will learn to appreciate the complementarity of an authentic Catholic interpretation Biblical Creation and a proper understanding of the Big Bang Theory. Using a variety of resources and approaches, students will be asked to reevaluate their thoughts about faith and science in light of what the Church teaches about Creation and the scientific acceptance regarding the Big Bang Theory.

This lesson and slides were created using Keynote. If you would like direct access to the animated and editable keynote slides, please reach out. The files were too large to upload in their original format.

(Notre Dame High School, Crowley LA)

Opening our Eyes to the Cosmos: A Catholic Scientific Perspective

  • Edit
  • Delete
During this two period lesson (totaling 1 hour 45 minutes), students will think critically about the cosmos within the fields of science and theology. They will reflect on and discuss that distinction using a powerpoint presentation and several video clips including various famous quotes and excerpts from philosophers, theologians, and scientists in the past and today. The lesson will model communication and students will communicate their thoughts and findings in a class discussion after collaborating with a partner.

Theories of God and the Multiverse: Metaphysics and Meaning

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson is an exercise in thinking through the types of questions science and theology ask and to what extent their answers can inform each other. This approach looks at theories of the Multiverse that have grown out of attempts to wed the mathematics of cosmology and quantum mechanics as well as models of God presented in the Old Testament and the Church Fathers.

Where Is God in the Multiverse?

  • Edit
  • Delete
This lesson is aimed at helping students learn how to have a charitable conversation/discussion regarding difficult scientific theories and how to apply scientific discoveries to Theological truths. Using a variety of resources and large-group discussions, students will be asked to work together to determine whether the scientific theory of multiverse challenges various Theological doctrines, including the existence of God, the Incarnation of Christ, and the efficacy of Christ’s death and resurrection.
